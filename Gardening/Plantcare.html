Watering Amounts

Watering needs for plants vary widely depending on type. Succulents and cacti need minimal water, about once every 2-3 weeks, allowing soil to dry completely between waterings. Medium-water plants like snake plants should be watered every 1-2 weeks, letting the top inch of soil dry out. High-water tropical plants such as ferns and peace lilies require weekly watering, with the soil kept consistently moist but not waterlogged. For vegetables, leafy greens need regular moisture, while root vegetables prefer deeper, less frequent watering.Trees and shrubs, especially young ones, need deep watering every 7-10 days, while mature trees can be watered monthly during dry periods. Always adjust based on factors like soil, climate, and growth stage.




Climates

Different climates affect watering needs significantly. In hot, dry climates, plants need more frequent, deep watering due to rapid evaporation, while in humid climates,less watering is required since the air retains moisture. In cool or temperate climates, plants need moderate watering with slower evaporation rates, and in tropical climates, consistent but not excessive watering is key due to high humidity. Always adjust based on local rainfall and temperature.

Plant food/Fertilizer

Plant food, or fertilizer, comes in three main types: organic (like compost), synthetic (chemical-based), and slow-release. Organic fertilizers enhance soil health and provide nutrients gradually, while synthetic options deliver quick results.Timing is crucial; fertilize during the growing season (spring and summer) according to plant needs, ensuring not to over-fertilize, which can harm plants.

How to protect from Bugs

To protect plants from bugs, regularly inspect them for signs of infestation and remove any affected leaves or pests by hand. Use organic insecticidal soaps or neem oil as natural repellents, and consider introducing beneficial insects like ladybugs. Maintaining healthy soil and proper watering can also boost plant resilience, making them less attractive to pests. Additionally, planting companion plants can deter harmful insects.
