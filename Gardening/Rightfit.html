About Gardening

Interests - Seasonal/Annual

Seasonal plants thrive at specific times of the year, like tulips in spring and sunflowers in summer, while annuals complete their life cycle in one growing season, such as marigolds and petunias.
Combining both can create vibrant, varied gardens that change with the seasons, providing continuous interest and color.

Garden size needed

The size of your garden depends on the types of plants you want to grow and your gardening goals.
a small herb or flower garden, a space of 10-20 square feet may suffice, while larger vegetable or fruit gardens typically require at least 100 square feet. Consider your available space, time for maintenance, and how much produce or flowers you wish to harvest.
